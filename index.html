Blockchain

It is a type of distributed and immutable ledger that contains transaction records. Transactions are grouped into blocks, and each block is linked to the previous one, creating a chain of blocks. This ensures the security and immutability of data, as any change in one block would affect all subsequent blocks.

Cryptographic keys

Cryptocurrencies use cryptographic key pairs, i.e., public key and private key, to ensure transaction security. The public key is the wallet address to which cryptocurrencies can be sent, while the private key is used to authorize transactions. The transaction value is signed using the private key and can be verified using the public key.

Transactions

Transactions on the blockchain network are typically public, permanent, and irreversible. Each transaction is broadcast to the network where it is verified by network nodes before being added to a block. Once added to a block, the transaction becomes immutable and visible to all network participants.

Mining

Our cryptocurrency harnesses the power of mining technology, a process vital to the integrity and operation of blockchain networks. Mining involves miners competing to solve intricate mathematical puzzles, known as Proof of Work (PoW), to validate transactions and add them to the blockchain. Through this mechanism, new units of the cryptocurrency are introduced into circulation. Mining serves as the backbone of our cryptocurrency network, ensuring its security and decentralization. Miners play a pivotal role in preventing fraudulent transactions and maintaining the integrity of the distributed ledger system. By expending computational resources to validate transactions, miners contribute to the immutability and trustworthiness of the blockchain. Furthermore, mining incentivizes network participation, as miners are rewarded with newly minted coins and transaction fees for their efforts. This incentive structure encourages a distributed network of participants, preventing any single entity from gaining control over the system. In essence, mining technology is the cornerstone of our cryptocurrency ecosystem, providing the framework for secure, decentralized transactions and the issuance of new coins.

Unlock Your Passive Income Potential

The staking process is extremely simple. All you need to do is store your Foxecoins in your wallet and leave them there for a certain period of time. In return for locking your coins, you receive rewards in the form of additional Foxecoins.

Staking is not only a way to earn additional funds, but also supports the stability and security of our blockchain network. The more people participate in the staking process, the stronger and more resistant our network becomes to attacks.
